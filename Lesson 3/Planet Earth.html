Planet Earth — Quick Guide

Planet Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. It has a diverse range of ecosystems, including forests, deserts, oceans, and polar regions. Earth is home to millions of species of plants and animals, as well as over 7 billion humans. The planet's atmosphere protects us from harmful solar radiation and helps regulate the climate. However, human activities such as deforestation, pollution, and greenhouse gas emissions are causing significant changes to Earth's environment. It's important for us to understand our planet and take steps to protect it for future generations.

Steps to Protect Our Planet

  1. Switch off lights when you leave a room
  2. Use a reusable water bottle every day
  3. Walk or cycle instead of travelling by car
  4. Plant a tree in your neighbourhood
  5. Spread awareness — talk to your friends!

Incredible Natural Wonders

  • Amazon Rainforest — home to 10% of all species on Earth
  • Great Barrier Reef — the largest coral reef, visible from space
  • Mount Everest — the highest point on Earth at 8,849 metres
  • Sahara Desert — the largest hot desert in the world
